2026 Academic, Group work Social Infrastructure Transformation

The project explores how the transformation of an existing structure can strengthen the surrounding neighborhood. Located in southern Malmö, the proposal reimagines the building Motetten, originally developed within the context of Sweden’s Million Programme. Rather than demolishing and rebuilding, the project works within the existing structure, giving new social and spatial value to what is already there.

At its core, a central square becomes the social heart of the community, encouraging encounters between people of different ages and backgrounds throughout the day. Around this space, workshops, a library, and sports facilities create opportunities for learning, activity, and everyday interaction.

By carefully adapting the existing building, the project preserves traces of its history while opening it up for new forms of community life and public use. The project envisions a cohabitat that makes residents feel safe, visible, and connected, while demonstrating how reuse and transformation can support a more sustainable and inclusive urban future.
